Primer Design

To amplify the TPS1 gene and the promoters ordered from Genscript (PGK1, TEF2, TDH2) in preparation for constructing a recombinant expression vector for transformation, corresponding homologous arm sequences were designed for the primers. Sequences homologous to the pYET vector and the TPS1 gene were amplified at both ends of the promoter, with additional sequences homologous to the pYET vector added downstream of the TPS1 gene for subsequent ligation.

1. TPS1 Amplification Primers, Connected with ADH2p Promoter

- Forward Primer (F): ctatatcgtaataccatcatATGACTACGGATAACGCTAAGGC
- Reverse Primer (R): tcgtgaaggcatgtttaaacTCAGTTTTTGGTGGCAGAGG

2. TPS1 Amplification Primers, Connected with TEF2, TDH3, and the Vector Lacking ADH2p Promoter:

- TPS with TDH3 Forward Primer(F): acttagtttcgaataaacacacataaacaaacaaaATGACTACGGATAACGCTAAGGC
- TPS with TEF2 Forward Primer(F): cggtcaacgaactataattaactaaacATGACTACGGATAACGCTAAGGC
- TPS with Vector Lacking ADH2p Promoter Reverse Primer(R): tcgtgaaggcatgtttaaacTCAGTTTTTGGTGGCAGAGG

3. TDH3 Promoter Amplification Primers

- TDH3 with Vector Lacking ADH2p Promoter Forward Primer(F): cggatccatttagcggccgctcagttcgagtttatcattatcaa
- TDH3 with TPS1 Gene Reverse Primer (R): tttgtttgtttatgtgtgtttattc

4. TEF2 Promoter Amplification Primers

- TEF2 with Vector Lacking ADH2p Promoter Forward Primer(F): cggatccatttagcggccgcattacccataaggttgtttgt
- TEF2 with TPS1 Gene Reverse Primer (R): Gtttagttaattatagttcgttgacc

Amplification of Target Gene TPS1

1. Experimental Objectives: To amplify the TPS1 gene from yeast S288c.

2. Experimental Steps:

  1. Pick an appropriate amount of yeast S288c and add 50 µL of 0.1 M NaOH. Let it sit for 10 minutes, then centrifuge and discard the supernatant. Add 50 µL of ddH2O and incubate in a 98°C metal bath for 10 minutes.
  2. Prepare a 10 µL PCR system (annealing temperature: 55°C):
Component Volume
ddH2O 3.3 µL
2x Phanta Buffer 5 µL
DMSO 0.4 µL
dNTP 0.2 µL
Forward Primer (F) 0.4 µL
Reverse Primer (R) 0.4 µL
Template (yeast) 0.2 µL
Phanta 0.1 µL

Gel Preparation and Electrophoresis

  1. Gel Preparation: 1% agarose TAE solution. Dissolve 4 g of agarose in 200 mL TAE by heating, then add 4 µL of nucleic acid dye after cooling slightly.
  2. Electrophoresis: Marker: DL8000; run at 180 V for 20 minutes.

Gel Recovery

  1. Cut out the target band from the gel and place it in a clean centrifuge tube, then weigh it.
  2. Add an equal volume of V1 gel solution to the tube, incubate at 55°C for 10 minutes, inverting 2-3 times to ensure complete melting.
  3. Briefly centrifuge to collect the liquid from the tube walls.
  4. Place the adsorption column in a collection tube, add the gel solution to the column, and centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 1 minute.
  5. Add 500 µL of wash solution W2 (with anhydrous ethanol) to the column, centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes.
  6. Repeat the previous step.
  7. After a final spin at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes, place the column in a clean centrifuge tube, open the lid, and let it dry for 5 minutes.
  8. Add 5 µL of TE to the adsorption membrane, let it sit at room temperature for 2 minutes, then centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 1 minute to collect the target gene and measure its concentration.

Experimental Results

Successfully amplified the TPS1 gene.

Connection and Transformation to Construct Recombinant Expression Vectors

1. Experimental Objectives:

  • To connect TEF2, TDH3 with the pYET vector lacking the ADH2p promoter and the TPS1 gene.
  • To connect the pYET vector retaining the ADH2p promoter with the TPS1 gene.
  • To transform into E. coli and select for ampicillin-resistant single colonies.

2. Experimental Steps

1. Connection

Multi-fragment Connection (TEF2 or TDH3, pYET lacking the ADH2p promoter, and TPS1 gene)

  • pYET vector: 0.02 × number of base pairs in the cloning vector (ng)
  • TPS1: 0.02 × number of base pairs in the TPS1 gene
  • Promoter: 0.02 × number of base pairs in the promoter

10 µl Connection System

  • pYET vector: 1 µl
  • TPS1: 2 µl
  • Promoter: 2 µl
  • ddH2O: 5 µl

Mix gently, incubate at 50°C for 15 min, then immediately place on ice.

Single-fragment Connection (retaining the ADH2p promoter on pYET)

  • pYET vector: 0.02 × number of base pairs in the cloning vector (ng)
  • TPS1: 0.04 × number of base pairs in the TPS1 gene

10 µl Connection System

  • pYET vector: 1 µl
  • TPS1: 2 µl
  • Promoter: 2 µl
  • ddH2O: 5 µl

Mix gently, incubate at 50°C for 15 min, then immediately place on ice.

2. Transformation

  1. Place the E. coli DH5α competent cells on ice to thaw.
  2. Add 10 µl of the recombinant product to 50 µl of competent cells, gently mix by flicking the tube, and incubate on ice for 30 min.
  3. Heat shock at 42°C for 30 s, then immediately place on ice for 3 min.
  4. Add 500 µl of LB liquid medium (without antibiotic) and incubate at 37°C for 1 h with shaking.
  5. Centrifuge at 5000 rpm for 5 min, discard most of the supernatant, and plate the remaining supernatant on LB medium with ampicillin, and culture overnight.

E. coli Plasmid Extraction

1. Experimental Objectives:

  • To obtain amplified plasmids through E. coli culture.
  • To acquire relatively pure target plasmids.
  • To conduct PCR verification of the target plasmids.

2. Experimental Steps

  1. Check Equipment:
    • Confirm that RNase A has been added to Buffer P1.
    • Verify that anhydrous ethanol is present in the Wash Solution.
    • Check for precipitation in Buffers P2 and P3.
  2. Cell Collection:
    • Take 1.5-5 mL of overnight cultured bacterial liquid, centrifuge at 8,000 × g for 2 minutes to collect cells, and discard the supernatant.
  3. Lysis:
    • Add 250 μl of Buffer P1 to the pellet and resuspend thoroughly.
    • Add 250 μl of Buffer P2, gently invert the tube 5-10 times to mix. Incubate at room temperature for 2-4 minutes.
  4. Centrifuge:
    • Add 350 μl of Buffer P3, gently invert the tube 5-10 times to mix.
  5. Collect Supernatant:
    • Centrifuge at 12,000 × g for 5-10 minutes. Transfer the supernatant to a binding column, and centrifuge at 8,000 × g for 30 seconds; discard the collection.
  6. Washing:
    • Add 500 μl of Wash Solution, centrifuge at 9,000 × g for 30 seconds, discard the liquid, and repeat once.
    • Centrifuge the empty binding column at 9,000 × g for 1 minute.
  7. Elution:
    • Place the binding column into a clean 1.5 mL centrifuge tube, add 50-100 μl of Elution Buffer to the center of the membrane, let sit at room temperature for 1 minute, then centrifuge for 1 minute. Save the DNA solution in the tube.

Colony PCR Verification

1. Experimental Objective:

To confirm the successful insertion of the TPS1 gene and promoters TEF2, TDH3 into the pYET plasmid.

2. Experimental Procedure:

  1. Take 3 positive clones, add 10 μl of sterile water, and mix.
  2. Use 1 μl of the bacterial liquid as PCR template. Add the remaining liquid to 500 μl of LB(Amp) medium and shake at 37°C for 5 hours.

10 μl PCR System:

  • ddH2O: 3.2 μl
  • Taq enzyme: 5 μl
  • DMSO: 0.4 μl
  • Primer F: 0.2 μl
  • Primer R: 0.2 μl
  • Template (bacterial liquid): 1 μl
  • Annealing temperature: 56°C

Perform agarose gel electrophoresis.

Select the culture liquid containing the correct positive clone and add to 3 mL of LB(Amp) medium, shaking for 5 hours.

3. Experimental Results:

  • PCR validation shows TDH3, TEF2 are ligated to pYET plasmid.
  • PCR validation shows TPS1 ligated to pYET plasmid without removal of the ADH2p promoter.

Measurement of TPS1 mRNA Expression

1. Purpose of the experiment

  • Compare the mRNA intensity using TDH3, TEF2, ADH2p, and blank groups.
  • To demonstrate overexpression of the TPS1 gene.
  • To demonstrate the utility of the Prometheus model recommended promoters TDH3 and TEF2.

2. Experimental Procedures

Total RNA extraction

  1. Add 200 μL of PBS to the yeast precipitate and resuspend, take 50 μL of RNA extraction.
  2. Add 950 μL Trizol, shake vigorously and place on ice.
  3. Add 200 μL chloroform, shake vigorously and mix well, let it stand at room temperature for 10 minutes, centrifuge at 16000 rpm for 20 minutes (4℃).
  4. Pipette the supernatant (500 μL) into a new centrifuge tube, add an equal volume of isopropanol, shake and let it stand at -20°C for 1 hour.
  5. Centrifuge at 16000 rpm for 20 minutes (4°C) and discard the supernatant.

Reverse Transcription

Genomic RNA removal: RNase-free centrifuge tube is configured with the following mixture, then blow and mix well, 42 ℃ for 2 minutes:

  • RNase-free ddH2O: 10 μL
  • 4x gDNA wiper Mix: 4 μL
  • Template RNA: 2 μL

Reverse transcription reaction system:

  • Add 4 μL of 5x HiScript III qRT SuperMix to the reaction solution in the first step, blow and mix well.

Reverse Transcription Reaction:

  • 37°C for 15 minutes
  • 85°C for 5 seconds

qPCR

Prepare 20 μL of system in a qPCR tube:

  • ddH2O: 7.2 μL
  • 2x ChamQ SYBR qPCR Master Mix (Without ROX): 10 μL
  • cDNA: 2 μL
  • Primer F: 0.4 μL
  • Primer R: 0.4 μL

qPCR was performed under the following conditions:

  • Pre-denaturation: 95°C for 30s
  • Cycle: 95°C for 10s, 60°C for 30s (40 cycles)
  • Melting curve: 95°C for 15s, 60°C for 60s, 95°C for 15s

3. Experimental Results:

The mRNA concentrations of the three promoters TDH3, TEF2, and ADH2 expressing TPS1 were determined using fluorescence quantitative PCR, and the relative expression of mRNAs expressing TPS1 from the four promoters was compared by Ct values. This allowed us to compare the relative strengths of the expression of the three promoters and demonstrate the elevation of the mRNA concentration with respect to the blank group.

Comparison of Resistance Growth of Yeast Transformants Expressing TPS1 Gene with Different Promoters

1. Experimental Objectives:

  • To verify that alginate is resistant to yeast growth.
  • To investigate whether there is a difference in resistance between different promoters expressing alginate.

2. Experimental Procedures:

  1. In the aseptic operating table, take 500 μL of bacterial solution from the seed vials of two experimental groups with different promoters and add it into 50 mL of culture medium, incubate at 37°C and 220 rpm for 40 hours for amplification and fermentation.
  2. Every 2-hour interval, take 1 mL culture solution and add it to the cuvette, measure the OD value of the bacterial solution.

3. Experimental Results:

By plotting the time and OD curves, the yeast with different promoters showed a better growth trend at thirty-seven degrees Celsius compared to the blank group.

The above figure shows the shaker flasks incubated for 12 hours. From left to right are TDH3, TEF2, pYET, and ADH2. A clear difference in the resilience of the blank group and the yeasts that achieved TPS1 overexpression to the unsuitable temperature can be seen.
